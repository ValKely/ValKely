Crime Data Analysis

Why this Analysis?

Crime data quality and insights are essential for law enforcement agencies to effectively allocate resources, detect patterns, and improve public safety. This analysis explores the integrity of the crime dataset and reveals key patterns related to crime types, locations, suspect demographics, and severity to support data-driven decision-making.

Data Quality & Integrity Checks

  • No duplicate records were detected, indicating a clean dataset.
  • All required fields have no missing (null) values, ensuring completeness.
  • Data types and value lengths were verified; no invalid or overly long entries found.

Key Insights from the Data

  • Crime Types Distribution: Theft is the most prevalent crime, followed by Assault and Fraud.
  • Location Hotspots: Downtown and Midtown are hotspots with the highest crime occurrences.
  • Severity Levels: Many crimes are Medium to High severity, needing urgent attention.
  • Suspect Demographics: The average suspect age is ~29 years, with balanced gender distribution.

Recommended Actions

  • Focus enforcement and preventive efforts on Downtown and Midtown hotspots.
  • Develop interventions targeting typical suspect demographics.
  • Prioritize Medium/High severity crimes for resource allocation.
  • Ensure continued data quality with robust validation processes.
